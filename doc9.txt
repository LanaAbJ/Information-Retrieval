Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is a barred spiral galaxy consisting of billions of stars, planets, and other celestial objects. It is part of the Local Group of galaxies, which includes the Andromeda galaxy. The Milky Way is estimated to be 100,000 light-years in diameter. At its center lies a supermassive black hole, which is surrounded by stars in an area called the galactic bulge. The galaxy has several spiral arms where star formation is most active
